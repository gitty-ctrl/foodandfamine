    • Damage to the world.



    .

    Scale

    About 1/3 of all food produced in the world is lost or wasted, which amounts to 1.3 billion tons of food every year.

    Country divide

    In developing countries, food waste often occurs due to poor infrastructure for storing and transporting food, while in developed countries it often happens due to overproduction and consumer waste.

    Climate Change

    Food waste is responsible for about 8% of global greenhouse gas emissions, making it a major contributor to climate change.

    Greenhouse gases

    If food waste were a country, it would be the third-largest emitter of greenhouse gases after the United States and China.

    Biodiversity

    Food waste also contributes to the loss of biodiversity, as farmers are forced to clear more land to produce more food to make up for the wasted food.

    Loss of resouces

    Food waste also wastes other resources used in food production, such as water, land, and energy.
    .

    Reducing food waste by just 25% could feed all of the 870 million people who suffer from hunger in the world.
