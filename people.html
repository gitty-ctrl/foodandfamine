    Economic

    Food waste has significant economic impacts on both individual households and entire countries. Wasted food represents lost income for farmers, food processors, and retailers. It also leads to increased costs for waste disposal and contributes to higher food prices. On an individual level, wasting food is essentially throwing away money.

    Environmental

    Food waste has a major impact on the environment, contributing to climate change and other environmental problems. It is responsible for the production of greenhouse gases, the use of land and water resources, and the pollution of air, water, and soil. Reducing food waste can help to mitigate some of these environmental impacts.

    Hunger&Poverty

    Food waste contributes significantly to hunger and poverty in the world. With 9% of the global population suffering from chronic hunger, and over 690 million people not having enough food to eat, the amount of food that is wasted could potentially solve the problem of hunger in the world.     Social Justice

    Food waste has significant social and humanitarian impacts, particularly in low-income communities. Food insecurity and hunger remain major problems in many parts of the world, and reducing food waste could help to alleviate these problems. Additionally, food waste has negative impacts on food banks and other organizations that work to provide food to those in need.

    Health

    Food waste has a negative impact on public health, particularly in low-income communities. The consumption of spoiled or contaminated food can lead to foodborne illnesses and other health problems. Additionally, the overproduction of food can lead to the use of pesticides and other harmful chemicals that can have negative impacts on human health.

    Ethics

    Wasting food raises ethical and moral questions about the distribution of resources and the treatment of people and animals involved in food production. When food is wasted, it is essentially taking resources away from those who need it. Additionally, food waste can contribute to overproduction, leading to issues such as overfishing and deforestation.
